The stock mechanics under the hood

Christian McCaffrey — the injury

$494 spot, wk18 · from $492 at IPO
  • Buy ▲ / Sell ▼price = P0 + slope × shares. Every buy walks it up, every sell down. 100 shares ⇒ +$200 spot.
  • InjuryWk14 he was OUT → every share paid $0. No special logic; the market just reprices as holders bail.
  • First to sell4 holders dump 25 each down the curve: first out nets +34% more than the last. First-in-first-out wins.

Where league money comes from

  • Dividendsthe only injection — new cash minted every Tuesday. Market mode: +$24.0k across the season; lineup just $13.2k (−45%).
  • Feesthe only leak — 1% burned per trade. It's the brake that stops zero-risk churn around the curve.
  • The curveis the house. A seller's cash comes from it; every remaining holder's mark drops as shares unwind. No player pays another directly.
  • Neteveryone's number drifts up ~+29% a season on dividends — the game is relative standing, not absolute.

The recommendation — pick the game you're shipping

Marketcasual · v1
Ship this as the default. Simplest to explain ("every share pays its points"), most forgiving, liveliest feed (most cash injected). Position choice is nearly free — 6.5% spread — so the whole game is trade timing & spotting mispriced players. The "all-QBs-win" fear is a myth: P0 is anchored to projections, so ROI is already position-flat.
Relativeneeds a fix
Currently over-corrects — it normalizes points on top of an economy that already normalizes via price, handing TE-loaders a +39% season vs QB's +23%. Keep it only as a deliberate "cheap positions are the meta" toggle, or recalibrate the factors toward 1.0.
Lineupserious · pro tier
The competitive mode: 21.5% skill spread, roster construction is everything, the QB stack collapses to +5%, and a tighter money supply makes price movement matter more. Costs a lineup-lock step — the natural paid/commissioner-league feature for the product.

Method. Real nflverse 2025 weekly PPR (weeks 1–18), top-200 player universe. Eight bot strategies trade a shared linear bonding curve via the production execute_trade; dividends post through the real mode-aware Tuesday job. Projections = actual season totals × fixed-seed forecast error (±28%), identical across all three modes so only scoring differs. Net worth = cash + holdings marked to the sell-curve. Caveats: bots are fixed across modes (a rational player would adapt — e.g. never stack QBs in lineup mode), and P0 uses actual-season projections, so real forecast error would widen every spread. Not investment advice; virtual league currency only.
